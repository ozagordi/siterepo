Genomics

Since Frederick Sanger developed his method for DNA sequencing, in the late '70s, science acquired a powerful technique to investigate the basic processes that govern life. As a matter of fact, one of the largest scientific endeavours of the last thirty years has been the sequencing of the human genome, the completion of which was announced in 2003 after a twenty year long international effort.

The sequencing technologies have advanced in the last few years at great speed, and some platforms are now able to produce gygabites of data in a single run. In order to make sense of this amount of sequences, a set of dedicated computational tools have been developed by computational biologists.

At enGene Statistics we can provide multi-year experience in the planning and analysis of your genomics experiment, be it a ChIP-Seq or RNA-Seq. Our team has been primarily involved in designing and perfroming experiments, as well as developing statistical and computational tools to analyse the results. Communication is of paramount importance to us, and we strive to document and explain the analysis we perform for you. Whether you have to write an internal report or a manuscript for a scientific journal, we assist you in describing your study.
